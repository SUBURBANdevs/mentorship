Our Strategy

There are many approaches to teen mentoring each with its pros and cons. MAVEN wishes to approach mentoring from an African social ideology perspective, where the whole community is involved in preparing the youth for adulthood.
To produce enlightened and skilled individuals who will impact the whole community positively. The formula is in:

Tried and tested parenting frameworks

Helping parents better understand the teen as an individual that is able to determine their own projectile in life

Providing schools and institutions with data and skills to empower the teen

Helping schools especially high schools to help shape individual talents as well us capability awareness of their students

Training mentors to best serve the teens

This being the most crucial link, most focus will go into building an alumni forum as well as mentors on boarding campaigns

Curation of data to improve the education system and employment market

Data is crucial in understanding our unique African situation and building systems to better improve services for the youth empowerment in Kenya and Africa.

Media & Advocacy

Use of media to shape mindset and lobbying for policies that create a favourable environment for teens to thrive.
